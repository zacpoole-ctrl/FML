Issue #1 • Q2 2026
The Q2 Draft Report
Rating every pick, projecting every score, and crowning an early favorite before a single Q2 film has opened.
Fantasy Movie League • March 2026

The Q2 auction is in the books. Twenty films. Five teams. One hundred dollars each. Below is a full grade on every pick — no film left unscored.

The Big Picture

Q2 is genuinely stacked. Mario Galaxy, Mando, and Toy Story 5 are three of the biggest commercial events of the entire year. The question was never whether this quarter would produce points — it was who paid the right price. Two teams stand out: Davis got the two best value picks of the draft, and Braden made the single biggest bet. Everyone else is fighting for third.

All 20 Picks Graded

Super Mario Galaxy Movie
Complex Productions (Braden)
Paid: $46 • Apr 1
A
Tracking at $145-160M domestic opening. Better heat than the 2023 original. No animated competition until Toy Story 5 in June. At $46 this is the right film to overpay for. Braden is looking at 1,200+ box office points if it runs like the first film.
The Drama
Box Office Bandits (Poole)
Paid: $21 • Apr 3
B
Zendaya and Robert Pattinson in an A24 rom-com by Kristoffer Borgli. Tracking ~$15M opening but the Letterboxd and Metacritic scores for this kind of A24 prestige comedy will be high. At $21 this is the right price. Solid floor pick with quality upside.
You, Me and Tuscany
Studio Six Productions (Crews)
Paid: $2 • Apr 10
B-
Halle Bailey in a Universal rom-com set in Italy. At $2 this is nearly a free roll. Rom-coms don't win Oscars but this demographic has commercial upside. Don't expect more than 80-120 total points, but the cost justifies it.
Mother Mary
Box Office Bandits (Poole)
Paid: $1 • Apr 17
A+
One dollar. Anne Hathaway, Michaela Coel, Hunter Schafer, FKA Twigs. David Lowery directing for A24. This is the steal of the draft. If it earns even five Oscar nominations, Poole just earned 5 points for $1. The PPD potential here is almost unfair.
Michael
Box Office Bandits (Poole)
Paid: $38 • Apr 24
C+
The riskiest expensive pick of the draft. MJ's real nephew playing MJ is fascinating. The estate's heavy involvement is the concern. Sanitized biopics get punished by critics. Oscar nominations for costume and score are plausible. But if Metacritic comes in under 60 and it opens soft, this is Poole's albatross at $38.
The Devil Wears Prada 2
The Final Cut (Aliyah)
Paid: $29 • May 1
B+
222 million trailer views in 24 hours. Meryl, Hathaway, Blunt, Tucci. Tracking $55M+ domestic opening. Female 35+ demographic is underserved in Q2. Ceiling could reach $600M worldwide. Acting nominations are a real possibility. Fair value at $29.
I Love Boosters
Studio Six Productions (Crews)
Paid: $20 • May 8
C+
SXSW reviews were good but not great — described as "won't be for everyone." That's a ceiling problem at $20. Limited box office profile and modest critical reception is the likely outcome. Crews's weakest pick by a wide margin.
Mortal Kombat 2
Fuck David Zaslav Pictures (Davis)
Paid: $1 • May 8
B
The first reboot made $83M worldwide on a modest budget. At $1, Davis gets real commercial upside for essentially nothing. Even if it only repeats those numbers, that's 83 box office points for $1. Davis's low-cost dart throws are the draft's most underrated story.
Obsession
Studio Six Productions (Crews)
Paid: $10 • May 15
A-
Biggest TIFF Midnight pickup ($15M+ to Focus Features). Critics are calling it one of the scariest films of the year. Letterboxd community is already obsessed with it. At $10 this is the sleeper steal of the draft — a horror film with genuine word-of-mouth and strong quality scoring upside.
In the Grey
The Final Cut (Aliyah)
Paid: $11 • May 15
C
War/survival drama with limited public profile. At $11 it's not a disaster but it's hard to see meaningful returns across multiple scoring categories. Aliyah needed a stronger pick here.
Star Wars: Mando and Grogu
Fuck David Zaslav Pictures (Davis)
Paid: $27 • May 22
A
Star Wars returns to theaters after seven years. Pedro Pascal, Jon Favreau, Sigourney Weaver. No competition on May 22. One projection has it at $898M worldwide. Even a conservative $500M run is 500 box office points for $27. Davis getting this and Toy Story 5 for a combined $64 is the value story of the draft.
Backrooms
Complex Productions (Braden)
Paid: $19 • May 29
B-
The internet horror phenomenon gets its feature film. Massive built-in online audience. Box office ceiling is limited but Letterboxd engagement will be strong. At $19 this is a reasonable mid-range bet — Braden's second-best pick after Mario.
The Mummy
Complex Productions (Braden)
Paid: $5 • May 29
B-
Lee Cronin directing a supernatural horror reimagining. Credible genre filmmaker, recognizable IP. At $5 this is smart value — low cost, modest expectations, and the potential to punch above its weight on Letterboxd. Braden's third-best pick.
Masters of the Universe
Box Office Bandits (Poole)
Paid: $23 • Jun 5
C
He-Man has failed on screen before and the IP has no recent cultural relevance. $23 feels like overpaying for that level of uncertainty. Poole's least defensible spend of the draft.
Scary Movie 6
The Final Cut (Aliyah)
Paid: $29 • Jun 5
C
The parody genre is commercially dead. Even if it opens on nostalgia, the Letterboxd and Metacritic ceilings for a Scary Movie film are brutal — 3.0 Letterboxd and 40 Metacritic at best. That's 12 combined quality points on a $29 spend. This and In the Grey are the picks that will haunt Aliyah's roster.
Disclosure Day
Studio Six Productions (Crews)
Paid: $46 • Jun 12
B+
Spielberg's UFO film, John Williams scoring, Emily Blunt leading. Box office projections at $450-550M worldwide. Real awards pedigree — if this gets the Oppenheimer treatment the nominations and wins add 30-60+ points at year-end. The risk is Spielberg's recent prestige films underperforming. The ceiling is the best of any non-blockbuster in the draft.
Toy Story 5
Fuck David Zaslav Pictures (Davis)
Paid: $37 • Jun 19
A+
Highest awareness tracking of any 2026 film. Tom Hanks back. Pixar coming off Hoppers outperforming expectations. Industry consensus has this crossing $1B worldwide. At $37, Davis got a film that should have cost $45+. Best pure value pick of the entire draft.
The Sheep Detectives
Complex Productions (Braden)
Paid: $30 • Jun 19
D+
The most baffling spend of the draft. $30 for a film with almost no public profile, no significant star power, and no clear awards angle. Mario carries Braden's roster. This pick does not. If it's a surprise breakout, Braden looks like a genius. The odds of that are low enough that this grade stands.
Supergirl
The Final Cut (Aliyah)
Paid: $21 • Jun 26
B-
Milly Alcock as Kara Zor-El, Craig Gillespie directing, Jason Momoa as Lobo. IMAX release. DCU rebuilding momentum post-Superman. At $21 this is fair value. If reviews land well, $400-500M worldwide is realistic.
Jackass 5
Fuck David Zaslav Pictures (Davis)
Paid: $1 • Jun 26
A
Johnny Knoxville. One dollar. Jackass Forever did $80M worldwide. Letterboxd loves this franchise unironically. No awards ceiling whatsoever, but at $1 the PPD on even a modest run is enormous. Davis picking up both Jackass 5 and Mother Mary-tier plays alongside two blockbusters is the draft strategy of the room.

Draft Grades by Team

Fuck David Zaslav Pictures (Davis) — A. Best draft of the room. Toy Story 5 at $37 is the best value pick. Mando at $27 is second best. Two $1 dart throws with real upside. The only team built to dominate both Q2 and the year standings.

Studio Six Productions (Crews) — B+. Disclosure Day at $46 is the biggest prestige gamble. Obsession at $10 is the sleeper steal. You Me and Tuscany at $2 is fine. I Love Boosters at $20 is the weak link. Built for awards season, not box office.

Complex Productions (Braden) — B. Mario at $46 is right. The Mummy at $5 and Backrooms at $19 are solid. The Sheep Detectives at $30 is inexplicable and could cost Braden the quarter.

Box Office Bandits (Poole) — C+. Mother Mary at $1 is the best PPD pick of the draft. The Drama at $21 is solid. Michael at $38 is risky. Masters of the Universe at $23 is the worst value spend on this roster.

The Final Cut (Aliyah) — C+. Prada 2 at $29 is justified. Supergirl at $21 is fine. In the Grey at $11 and Scary Movie 6 at $29 are both poor value. Aliyah needed different films in those two slots.
